B2B Payments: From Ambiguity to Alignment

How I transformed an undefined payments initiative into a scalable, user-centered roadmap that established the foundation for long-term platform growth.

Project Overview

I defined the vision, roadmap, and execution for a three-week design spike. A loosely scoped checkout redesign evolved into a strategic, scalable payments solution. I reframed the problem, aligned cross-functional partners, and established the foundation to scale B2B payments into a long-term growth initiative.

The Problem

The legacy payments experience was fragmented, manual, and disconnected, limiting supplier adoption and constraining business growth. Low automation, limited payment methods, and an outdated UI eroded confidence and prevented scale.

Only 15% of suppliers had payments enabled, highlighting a critical missed opportunity to unlock revenue.

Opportunity

Redesign payments to unlock adoption, drive self-service, and scale revenue by connecting ordering, checkout, and invoicing into a seamless, growth-driving experience.

The existing payments experience was a single screen with unclear messaging that failed to meet the needs of our diverse user segments.

Reframing the Problem

I reframed the challenge as an opportunity to learn directly from our users. Instead of focusing on immediate delivery, the initiative became a strategic foundation for future work. Checkout served as a lens to uncover opportunities, validate assumptions, and shape long-term product direction, shifting the focus from fixing a checkout to designing a scalable end-to-end payments experience.

How might we create a payments experience that instills confidence in both buyers and suppliers, while simplifying complexity and driving growth?

Design Approach

We ran a three-week design spike to rapidly turn ambiguity into a clear vision. A small, cross-functional team defined goals, constraints, and success criteria, then used high-fidelity prototypes to test assumptions, uncover insights, and align stakeholders on the path forward.

Cross-Functional Collaboration

Twice-daily check-ins and design reviews ensured visibility, reinforced accountability, and aligned decisions with both user and business needs. This approach allowed partners to anticipate challenges, de-risk constraints, and streamline downstream development.

Strategic Leadership

When scope drifted due to GTM partners focusing on edge cases, I realigned the team by clarifying the spike's goals of rapid discovery, validation, and strategic vision. Refocusing the team rebuilt trust and kept efforts aligned with high-impact outcomes.

Design & Validation

With our project objectives solidified, I built functional prototypes for testing against our target users. As we were still in a highly exploratory phase, moderated testing provided deep insights into what users did and why, allowing us to validate against our hypotheses.

Participants were recruited from our ICP segment who had previously used NuORDER Payments and completed our CES survey. Buyers: n=10; Brands: n=5.

Stakeholder Alignment

The design spike was successful in clarifying the problem space, aligning Product, Design, and Engineering, and surfacing critical user insights. Testing revealed where we could iterate quickly, where clarity allowed us to plan confidently, and where further investigation was needed.

Execution

With leadership aligned, I translated the vision into actionable next steps for the team, balancing long-term strategy with the need for rapid MVP delivery. I worked closely with PMs, engineers, and stakeholders to navigate shifting priorities, unblock teams, and maintain alignment across disciplines.

Driving Scalable Growth

Iterations continued to enhance the experience and reveal opportunities for cross-product coordination and payments-driven growth. My solution introduced reusable components and patterns setting the foundation for scalable experiences across the product ecosystem.

The redesigned payments experience and supporting patterns were built with modular components that saw broad adoption across both the B2B and systems teams.

Business Impact

The reimagined payments experience drives business growth by connecting ordering, checkout, and invoicing into a singular, seamless flow. This integration unlocked exponential growth in transaction volume and increased self-service adoption, nearly doubling active invoice usage. Ultimately, this work positions the business to scale payments efficiently, capture high-value revenue, and build a more profitable B2B ecosystem.

Reflection

This project reinforced that effective design leadership turns ambiguity into alignment. By framing the right problem, uniting teams early, and designing for scale, we built the foundation for B2B payments and demonstrated how a design-led spike can drive both clarity and long-term strategy.
