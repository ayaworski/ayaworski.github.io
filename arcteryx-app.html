The Problem

The mobile app was a strategic lever for DTC growth, offering a faster, more native experience than mobile web. Early results were promising, but eventually engagement plateaued, retention fell, and uninstall rates rose.

Discovery

To uncover root causes, I led a focused discovery phase. This included empathy mapping to understand motivations and content behaviour patterns, and journey mapping to identify friction and opportunities.

I centered the narrative on Sarah, a mobile-first Arc'teryx loyalist who engages deeply with social and brand content. She represents the core DTC customer who values outdoor culture and community.

The app experienced a significant drop in user engagement over time. Journey mapping revealed opportunities for innovation and personalization, enabling a shift to a customer-centric perspective.

Reframing the Problem

The app was functional but forgettable. It behaved like a lightweight version of the website rather than a destination for daily engagement. Through user research, it was clear that customers expected an engaging and distinctive Arc'teryx experience. Instead, the app offered minimal content, no personalization, and no compelling reason to return. The problem was not conversion. The problem was stickiness.

How might we create an app that drives engagement while supporting revenue growth?

Strategic Alignment

This shifted the initiative from a basic app redesign to building a content-driven, personalized brand experience. By partnering with social, retail, and brand teams, we identified opportunities to repurpose existing social content, stories, and community events in a way that felt natural to the app.

Ideation & Prototyping

Once direction was clear, prototypes helped communicate the vision. I shared progress early and often to test ideas, expose technical constraints, and gather actionable stakeholder feedback.

Business Impact

The redesigned experience drove measurable improvements in engagement, retention, and conversion. Increased time in the app translated into higher order volume and stronger customer loyalty.

The Explore Feed became the core engagement engine, validating the strategic shift toward personalization and content.

Reflection

This project reinforced that effective design leadership turns ambiguity into alignment. By framing the right problem and uniting cross-functional teams around a shared vision, I delivered a solution that significantly improved user engagement, conversion, and retention.
